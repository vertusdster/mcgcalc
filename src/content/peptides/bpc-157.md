## What is BPC-157?

BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound-157) is a pentadecapeptide consisting of 15 amino acids. It is a partial sequence of body protection compound derived from human gastric juice. Researchers have studied BPC-157 extensively for its potential role in accelerating wound healing, tendon and ligament repair, and gastrointestinal protection.

## Research Dosing Parameters

In research settings, BPC-157 is commonly studied at the following parameters:

- **Typical dose range:** 200–500 mcg per injection
- **Common vial size:** 5 mg
- **Reconstitution:** 2–5 mL bacteriostatic water per vial
- **Injection frequency:** Once or twice daily in research models

## How to Reconstitute BPC-157

1. Draw the desired volume of bacteriostatic water into a sterile syringe
2. Inject slowly along the side of the vial — do not shake
3. Gently swirl until fully dissolved
4. Store reconstituted peptide refrigerated at 2–8°C
5. Use within 30 days once reconstituted
