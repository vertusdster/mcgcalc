## What is Semaglutide?

Semaglutide is a glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1) receptor agonist. It is a 31-amino acid peptide analogue of human GLP-1 with approximately 94% sequence homology. Researchers study semaglutide for its effects on blood glucose regulation, appetite suppression, and metabolic function.

## Research Dosing Parameters

- **Typical starting dose:** 250 mcg per week
- **Common vial size:** 5 mg
- **Reconstitution:** 2–5 mL bacteriostatic water
- **Titration:** Doses are typically titrated upward over several weeks in research protocols

## How to Reconstitute Semaglutide

1. Allow the vial to reach room temperature before reconstituting
2. Inject bacteriostatic water slowly down the side of the vial
3. Do not shake — gently roll or swirl until dissolved
4. The solution should be clear and colorless
5. Store reconstituted peptide at 2–8°C, use within 28 days
