## What Is Semaglutide?

Semaglutide is a **glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1) receptor agonist** — a 31–amino-acid analog of the human incretin hormone GLP-1, acylated with a C18 fatty di-acid chain that binds albumin and extends its half-life to roughly one week, enabling once-weekly injection. Unlike the research peptides on this site, semaglutide is an **FDA-approved medication** marketed as Ozempic and Rybelsus (type 2 diabetes) and Wegovy (chronic weight management), developed by Novo Nordisk.

Mechanistically, it activates the GLP-1 receptor to enhance glucose-dependent insulin secretion, suppress glucagon, slow gastric emptying, and increase satiety through central appetite pathways. It is the most extensively studied compound in its class, and its dual-incretin successor is [Tirzepatide](/peptides/tirzepatide). Because it is an approved drug backed by large randomized trials, the evidence base below is fundamentally stronger than that of unapproved research peptides.

### Overview

The semaglutide evidence base is exceptionally deep, spanning the **SUSTAIN** (diabetes), **STEP** (weight management), **PIONEER** (oral formulation), and **SELECT** (cardiovascular) programs — collectively tens of thousands of randomized participants published in NEJM, _The Lancet_, and JAMA. Research clusters around four themes: glycemic control in type 2 diabetes, weight reduction, cardiovascular outcomes, and formulation (injectable vs oral). Evidence maturity is high across all four, in contrast to the preclinical-only status of most peptides indexed on this site.

### Type 2 Diabetes Glycemic Control (SUSTAIN)

The SUSTAIN program established semaglutide's glycemic efficacy. [Sorli et al. (2017)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28110911/) (SUSTAIN 1) showed that semaglutide monotherapy significantly reduced HbA1c and body weight versus placebo in drug-naïve type 2 diabetes. In a head-to-head trial, [Pratley et al. (2018)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29397376/) (SUSTAIN 7) reported greater reductions in HbA1c and body weight than dulaglutide, positioning semaglutide as a best-in-class GLP-1 receptor agonist for glycemic control.

### Weight Management (STEP)

The STEP program extended semaglutide to obesity without diabetes. The landmark trial [Wilding et al. (2021)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33567185/) (STEP 1) reported a mean body-weight reduction of approximately 14.9% at 68 weeks with once-weekly 2.4 mg, versus 2.4% with placebo — the largest weight loss of any obesity pharmacotherapy at the time, as summarized in the review by [Chao et al. (2023)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34942372/). This result underpinned the Wegovy approval for chronic weight management.

### Cardiovascular Outcomes (SELECT, SUSTAIN-6)

Cardiovascular benefit distinguishes semaglutide from earlier weight-loss drugs. [Marso et al. (2016)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27633186/) (SUSTAIN-6) first showed a significant reduction in major adverse cardiovascular events in high-risk type 2 diabetes (hazard ratio 0.74). [Lincoff et al. (2023)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37952131/) (SELECT) then demonstrated a 20% reduction in cardiovascular events in patients with obesity and established cardiovascular disease **without** diabetes — a pivotal finding that reframed semaglutide as a cardiovascular-risk-reduction therapy, not merely a metabolic one.

### Oral Formulation and Comparative Efficacy (PIONEER, head-to-head)

Semaglutide is also the first GLP-1 receptor agonist available orally. [Husain et al. (2019)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31185157/) (PIONEER 6) established the cardiovascular safety of oral semaglutide in high-risk type 2 diabetes. In direct comparison with the dual agonist tirzepatide, [Aronne et al. (2025)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40353578/) (SURMOUNT-5) and [Frías et al. (2021)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34170647/) (SURPASS-2) reported greater weight loss and HbA1c reduction with [tirzepatide](/peptides/tirzepatide), placing semaglutide as a highly effective but not maximal option within the incretin class.

### Methodological Observations

The semaglutide literature is characterized by large, well-powered, placebo- and active-controlled RCTs with multi-year follow-up and hard clinical endpoints (HbA1c, body weight, cardiovascular events). This is a marked contrast to the small rodent studies typical of research peptides. Safety data are drawn from the same trial infrastructure; [Smits and Van Raalte (2022)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34305810/) reviewed the comprehensive safety profile across the SUSTAIN and PIONEER programs.

### Safety, GI Effects, and Limitations

- **Gastrointestinal effects** — Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and constipation are the most common adverse effects, driven by delayed gastric emptying, and are the leading cause of discontinuation.
- **Boxed warning** — A class thyroid C-cell tumor signal in rodents underlies a contraindication in personal/family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma or MEN 2.
- **Lean-mass consideration** — A portion of the weight lost is lean mass, a topic of ongoing study across the incretin class.
- **Access and formulation** — Compounded and research "reconstitution" semaglutide differs from the approved, quality-controlled product; potency and purity of non-pharmacy sources are not assured.
- **Durability** — Weight regain is common after discontinuation, indicating that benefit depends on continued therapy.

## Reconstitution Mathematics

Some semaglutide is supplied as a lyophilized powder requiring reconstitution. The interactive calculator on this page handles all the arithmetic — but understanding the underlying formula is useful for verification:

```
Concentration (mg/mL) = Vial Size (mg) ÷ BAC Water Added (mL)
Dose Volume (mL)      = Desired Dose (mg) ÷ Concentration (mg/mL)
Syringe Units (IU)    = Dose Volume (mL) × 100   [for U-100 insulin syringe]
```

**Common reconstitution examples:**

| Vial Size | BAC Water | Concentration | 250 mcg dose |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| 5 mg      | 2 mL      | 2.5 mg/mL     | **10 IU**    |
| 5 mg      | 1 mL      | 5.0 mg/mL     | **5 IU**     |
| 10 mg     | 2 mL      | 5.0 mg/mL     | **5 IU**     |

## Common Dosing Parameters

> The following reflects the FDA-approved titration schedule for injectable semaglutide. Actual dosing must be directed by a prescribing clinician.

Approved semaglutide dosing uses a gradual titration to limit gastrointestinal side effects. IU values below assume a 5 mg vial reconstituted in 2 mL BAC water (2.5 mg/mL):

| Phase | Weekly Dose | Injection Volume | Syringe Units (IU) | Typical Duration |
|-------|-------------|------------------|--------------------|------------------|
| Initiation | 0.25 mg | 0.10 mL | **10 IU** | Weeks 1–4 |
| Escalation | 0.5 mg | 0.20 mL | **20 IU** | Weeks 5–8 |
| Escalation | 1.0 mg | 0.40 mL | **40 IU** | Weeks 9–12 |
| Escalation | 1.7 mg | 0.68 mL | **68 IU** | Weeks 13–16 |
| Maintenance | 2.4 mg | 0.96 mL | **96 IU** | Ongoing (Wegovy) |

**Key observations from the labeling and trials:**
- The 0.25 mg starting dose is sub-therapeutic by design — it exists to build GI tolerance
- Diabetes maintenance (Ozempic) commonly targets 0.5–2.0 mg; weight management (Wegovy) targets 2.4 mg
- Titration pace should slow if GI effects are significant, per the approved labeling
- A 2.4 mg dose at 2.5 mg/mL nearly fills a 1 mL syringe — verify draw volume against syringe capacity

## Stability & Storage

| State | Temperature | Duration |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| Lyophilized (dry powder) | −20 °C (freezer) | 24+ months |
| Reconstituted in BAC water | 2–8 °C (refrigerator) | Up to 28 days |
| Reconstituted, room temp | 20–25 °C | 48 hours maximum |

Bacteriostatic water (BAC water, 0.9% benzyl alcohol) is the standard diluent for reconstitution because benzyl alcohol acts as a preservative. Approved pre-filled pens have their own manufacturer storage instructions, which differ from reconstituted powder.

**Reconstitution procedure (for lyophilized product):**

1. Allow vial to reach room temperature (~15 minutes)
2. Wipe septum with 70% isopropyl alcohol
3. Draw desired BAC water volume into a sterile syringe
4. Insert needle at a 45° angle and inject **slowly along the vial wall** — do not inject directly onto the lyophilized cake
5. Gently swirl (do not shake or vortex) until fully dissolved
6. Label vial with date, concentration, and store per table above

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How many units do I draw for 250 mcg of Semaglutide?**

It depends on your reconstitution ratio. With a 5 mg vial in 2 mL BAC water (2.5 mg/mL concentration), a 250 mcg dose = **10 units** on a U-100 insulin syringe. For a 0.5 mg dose it would be **20 units**, and for the 2.4 mg maintenance dose, **96 units**. Use the calculator above for your specific setup.

**What is the difference between Semaglutide and Tirzepatide?**

Semaglutide is a single-receptor GLP-1 agonist; [Tirzepatide](/peptides/tirzepatide) is a dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist. In head-to-head trials (SURPASS-2, SURMOUNT-5), tirzepatide produced greater HbA1c and weight reductions, though both are highly effective. Use the [multi-peptide mode](/peptide-calculator) if you need to model both.

**Is Semaglutide the same as Ozempic and Wegovy?**

Yes — semaglutide is the active ingredient. Ozempic and Rybelsus are approved for type 2 diabetes, while Wegovy is approved for weight management at the higher 2.4 mg maintenance dose. Rybelsus is the oral formulation; Ozempic and Wegovy are injectable.

**How long does reconstituted Semaglutide last in the refrigerator?**

Reconstituted in bacteriostatic water and stored at 2–8 °C, semaglutide is generally stable for up to 28 days. Approved pre-filled pens follow separate manufacturer dating. Always use a fresh alcohol swab before each draw, and discard cloudy or discolored solution.

**Why does Semaglutide dosing start so low?**

The 0.25 mg initiation dose is designed to build tolerance to gastrointestinal side effects (nausea, vomiting), not to treat — clinical efficacy comes at higher maintenance doses. This gradual titration is a defining feature of the approved labeling and was used across the SUSTAIN and STEP trials.
